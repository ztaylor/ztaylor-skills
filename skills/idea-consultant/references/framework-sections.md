# Framework Sections Reference

This document provides examples of sections and checklist items for different idea types. Use as inspiration—don't copy rigidly. Every idea is unique; generate sections that fit the specific concept.

---

## Universal Sections

These apply to almost every business idea:

### Legal & Entity Structure

- [ ] **Business entity type**: LLC, S-Corp, C-Corp, sole proprietorship—choose based on liability, taxes, and future plans
- [ ] **State of incorporation**: Consider Delaware for future fundraising, home state for simplicity
- [ ] **Operating agreement**: Even for single-member LLC, document ownership and procedures
- [ ] **Business licenses**: Research federal, state, and local requirements
- [ ] **DBA registration**: If operating under a name different from legal entity
- [ ] **EIN**: Obtain from IRS for tax purposes and banking
- [ ] **Business bank account**: Separate personal and business finances from day one
- [ ] **Contracts**: Customer agreements, vendor contracts, partnership agreements
- [ ] **Terms of service**: If applicable for digital products/services
- [ ] **Privacy policy**: Required for any data collection

### Branding & Positioning

- [ ] **Brand name**: Availability check (domain, trademark, social handles)
- [ ] **Positioning statement**: Who you serve, what problem you solve, why you're different
- [ ] **Brand voice**: How you communicate (formal/casual, playful/serious)
- [ ] **Visual identity**: Logo, colors, typography, imagery style
- [ ] **Brand guidelines**: Document standards for consistency
- [ ] **Domain registration**: Primary and defensive registrations
- [ ] **Social handles**: Claim across relevant platforms

### Financial Planning

- [ ] **Startup costs**: One-time expenses before revenue
- [ ] **Operating costs**: Monthly/annual recurring expenses
- [ ] **Revenue model**: How you make money (units, subscriptions, services, etc.)
- [ ] **Unit economics**: Cost per unit, margin per unit, contribution margin
- [ ] **Break-even analysis**: When do you become profitable?
- [ ] **Cash flow projection**: Month-by-month for first 12-24 months
- [ ] **Funding requirements**: How much do you need, when, from where?
- [ ] **Accounting system**: QuickBooks, Xero, Wave—choose and set up
- [ ] **Tax planning**: Quarterly estimates, deduction tracking, professional help

### Pricing Strategy

- [ ] **Cost-plus analysis**: What are your actual costs per unit/customer?
- [ ] **Competitive analysis**: What do alternatives charge?
- [ ] **Value-based pricing**: What is it worth to the customer?
- [ ] **Price testing**: How will you validate pricing before committing?
- [ ] **Discounting strategy**: When and how much, if ever?
- [ ] **Price anchoring**: Premium tiers to make standard seem reasonable?

## Physical Product Sections

### Product Design & Specifications

- [ ] **Core specifications**: Size, weight, materials, components
- [ ] **Design iterations**: Sketches, CAD, prototypes
- [ ] **User testing**: Prototype feedback before manufacturing
- [ ] **Design for manufacturing**: Simplify for production efficiency
- [ ] **Packaging requirements**: Protection, presentation, unboxing experience
- [ ] **Compliance marks**: CE, FCC, UL—what certifications are needed?
- [ ] **Sustainability**: Materials, recyclability, environmental impact

### Manufacturing

- [ ] **Domestic vs. overseas**: Trade-offs of cost, quality, lead time, MOQ
- [ ] **Manufacturer research**: Alibaba, Maker's Row, ThomasNet, trade shows
- [ ] **Sample orders**: Test quality before committing to production
- [ ] **MOQ negotiation**: Minimum order quantities and how to reduce them
- [ ] **Production timeline**: Lead times from order to delivery
- [ ] **Quality standards**: Specifications, inspection criteria, defect rates
- [ ] **Backup suppliers**: Never depend on a single manufacturer

### Inventory & Fulfillment

- [ ] **Inventory management**: Tracking system, reorder points, safety stock
- [ ] **Storage solution**: Home, warehouse, 3PL, Amazon FBA
- [ ] **Fulfillment process**: Pick, pack, ship workflow
- [ ] **Shipping carriers**: USPS, UPS, FedEx, DHL—negotiate rates
- [ ] **Packaging materials**: Boxes, mailers, fill, tape, labels
- [ ] **Returns process**: Policy, logistics, restocking

## Software/SaaS Sections

### Technical Architecture

- [ ] **Technology stack**: Languages, frameworks, databases
- [ ] **Hosting infrastructure**: Cloud provider, serverless vs. containers
- [ ] **Scalability planning**: How will you handle growth?
- [ ] **Third-party services**: Auth, payments, email, analytics
- [ ] **API design**: If applicable, external integrations
- [ ] **Data model**: Core entities and relationships

### Development Process

- [ ] **MVP scope**: What's the minimum to validate the idea?
- [ ] **Feature prioritization**: Framework for deciding what to build
- [ ] **Development methodology**: Agile, sprints, continuous deployment
- [ ] **Code quality**: Testing, code review, documentation
- [ ] **Version control**: Git workflow, branching strategy
- [ ] **CI/CD pipeline**: Automated testing and deployment

### Security & Compliance

- [ ] **Authentication**: How users prove identity
- [ ] **Authorization**: What users can access
- [ ] **Data encryption**: At rest and in transit
- [ ] **Compliance requirements**: GDPR, CCPA, SOC2, HIPAA if applicable
- [ ] **Security audits**: Regular vulnerability assessments
- [ ] **Incident response**: Plan for breaches or outages

## Service Business Sections

### Service Design

- [ ] **Service offerings**: What exactly do you provide?
- [ ] **Scope definition**: What's included, what's not
- [ ] **Delivery method**: In-person, remote, hybrid
- [ ] **Service levels**: Response times, availability, guarantees
- [ ] **Customization**: Standardized vs. bespoke offerings

### Client Management

- [ ] **Sales process**: Discovery, proposal, close
- [ ] **Onboarding**: How new clients get started
- [ ] **Communication**: Channels, frequency, documentation
- [ ] **Project management**: Tools, status updates, milestones
- [ ] **Feedback loops**: Regular check-ins, satisfaction surveys
- [ ] **Offboarding**: Transition when engagement ends

### Pricing Models

- [ ] **Hourly rates**: Simple but caps earning potential
- [ ] **Project-based**: Fixed fee for defined scope
- [ ] **Retainer**: Recurring fee for ongoing access
- [ ] **Value-based**: Priced on outcomes, not inputs
- [ ] **Productized services**: Standardized offerings at fixed prices

## Marketplace Sections

### Supply Side (Sellers/Providers)

- [ ] **Acquisition strategy**: How do you get your first 50 suppliers?
- [ ] **Onboarding process**: Make it easy to list/join
- [ ] **Quality standards**: Vetting, ratings, removal criteria
- [ ] **Incentive structure**: Why would they choose your platform?
- [ ] **Retention**: Keep good suppliers active and engaged

### Demand Side (Buyers/Customers)

- [ ] **Acquisition strategy**: How do you get your first 100 buyers?
- [ ] **Search/discovery**: How do buyers find what they need?
- [ ] **Trust signals**: Reviews, verification, guarantees
- [ ] **Conversion optimization**: Reduce friction in transactions

### Platform Mechanics

- [ ] **Chicken-and-egg strategy**: Which side do you build first?
- [ ] **Take rate**: Your cut of transactions
- [ ] **Payment processing**: Escrow, split payments, payouts
- [ ] **Dispute resolution**: When things go wrong
- [ ] **Fraud prevention**: Fake listings, bad actors
